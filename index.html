One of my favourite hobbies includes 3d printing! Where you can make or find 3d models and using different types of materials, most commonly plastics like PLA, TPU, and ABS you can then print out that model layer by layer. For 3d printing you first start off with a 3d model, that you then have to input into a slicer, which breaks down that model layer by layer so then the printer knows how to print it, this then produces a g-code, which in my case I then export onto a USB, and then upload it to my printer, then I preheat my printer and insert a filament of my choice, filament refers to your printing material. The most common is PLA (polylactic acid) which is a form of rigid plastic, compared to others like TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane) which is more of a soft plastic like silicone, which is more flexible. Then I finally program my printer to print the model, this is the longest part as simple prints take around 1-3 hours to print, the more complicated the longer it takes.

My longest 3d print to this date has been 17 hours, in which I 3d printed a Julius Caesar pencil holder. It holds pencils by stab wounds in his back. Some honourable mentions are: T-Rex skull (10 hours), and a lattice (6 hours). Luckily a lot of 3d models can be found free online, as while modeling and creating your own is fun, it takes a lot of time, patience, and practice if you don’t have access to a 3d scanner. For example, I had to try and replicate a part for our washing machine that had broken off, it took a lot of measurements and trial an error as I had to do all the modelling and measurements by hand and since the piece that broke was quite big, it took along time to print, but after 3 different prototypes I finally had it correct.
